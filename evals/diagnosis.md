## Pre-rebaseline diagnosis (preserved for context)

Run: 2026-04-27, 68-query golden set with **both corpora** narrowed to
section/page granularity:
- 27 of 33 NSIDC rows carry `expected_sections` or `expected_pages`.
- 15 of 35 docsearch rows carry `expected_sections` (added after the
  pilot review caught a multi-section-page over-counting bug — see
  "Pilot review findings" below).
- The remaining 6 NSIDC `product_disambiguation` rows + 20 single-topic

MiniLM-L6 + IDF-overlap + RRF(k=60), no reranker, no tuning.

## Verdict

**All three metrics are below the bar.** This is the expected outcome of
replacing coarse URL-level matching with section/page-level matching:
the previous numbers were inflated by counting "any chunk from the
right page/PDF" as a hit, including chunks from unrelated sections of
the same page. Tightening reveals what the retrieval actually delivers
at section precision.

| Metric | URL-only (n=68) | After NSIDC narrowing | After **both** narrowings | Bar | Pass? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| recall@5 | 0.853 | 0.691 | **0.691** | 0.70 | ✗ (just below) |
| hit@1    | 0.574 | 0.397 | **0.324** | 0.50 | ✗ |
| MRR      | 0.698 | 0.527 | **0.480** | 0.55 | ✗ |

Per-corpus split (current state — both narrowings applied):

| | recall@5 | hit@1 | MRR |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| docsearch (n=35) | 0.829 | 0.400 | 0.583 |
| nsidc (n=33) | 0.545 | 0.242 | 0.371 |

The docsearch hit@1 dropped from 0.543 → 0.400 after applying
`expected_sections` to multi-section module pages (`user_guide/icesat2.html`,
`api_reference/*.html`, etc.). `recall@5` is unchanged on docsearch
(0.829) — the answers we found before, we still find. Only top-1
ranking changed, because the auto-label was previously over-counting
top-1 URL matches that landed on wrong sections.

95% Wilson CIs (n=68, both narrowings):
- recall@5 = 0.691 → [0.57, 0.79]
- hit@1 = 0.324 → [0.22, 0.44]
- MRR ≈ 0.48 → roughly [0.36, 0.59]

These are wide but **honest**: at section precision, the agent gets the
right chunk at #1 about a third of the time and somewhere in top 5 about
70% of the time.

## Pilot review findings (rows 1-5, completed)

The user reviewed the first 5 rows by hand and rated each returned
chunk `correct` / `partial` / `wrong`. Row 5 (cnf confidence filter)
revealed the docsearch multi-section-page bug:

- **Auto-label said hit@1**: rank-1 chunk's URL was in `expected_urls`.
- **Human said r1=wrong**: the rank-1 chunk was from `user_guide/icesat2.html`
  but in the **ATL24 query parameters** section, not the cnf section.
- **Diagnosis**: `user_guide/icesat2.html` is a long page covering
  multiple ATL products; URL-only matching counts wrong-section chunks
  as hits.
- **Fix applied**: added `expected_sections` to 15 docsearch rows that
  target this kind of multi-section page.

Row 2 (atl06x surface fit) revealed a feature gap: the user provided a
human-truth URL with an anchor fragment (`#atl06-atl06x`). The
ingest now splits these fragments into separate `urls` + `sections`
entries automatically.

Pilot summary: 4 of 5 queries `overall: correct`, 1 `partial`. Routing
all `keep` (the 5 rows are SlideRule-flavored docsearch queries; cross-
corpus routing decisions will get more interesting on NSIDC rows).

## What this changes

1. **The "directionally true but uncertain" hit@1 finding from the prior
   run is now resolved in the wrong direction.** With section/page
   labeling, the lower bound of the hit@1 CI is below 0.50, so the
   retrieval is genuinely not clearing the bar yet — not just "close to
   it within sampling noise."
2. **The Tier 1-2 levers in [LongTermIdeas.md](../LongTermIdeas.md) are
   now urgent, not just nice-to-have.** A cross-encoder reranker on
   top-20 is the single most likely lever to close the section-level
   gap; `source_product` filtering and category boosting (atbd
   over user_guide on "how does X work" queries) are the
   supporting moves the per-query audit points to.
3. **The previous Option B headline ("retrieval clears the bar, move
   to Option C") is paused.** Architecture POC is on hold until the
   first or second lever moves the section-level numbers back across
   the line. There's no value in load-testing a retrieval that
   misranks 75% of NSIDC queries at top-1.

**The pattern is unmistakable**: `nsidc/algorithm` going from URL-level
hit@1=0.500 to section-level hit@1=0.000 is the same retrieval, viewed

correct on the first hit. **A cross-encoder reranker on the top-20 is
specifically what closes this kind of gap.**

## Diagnosis — what's actually going wrong

**Most-fixable single failure**: `nsidc` queries that name a specific
product retrieve chunks from the wrong product. On "ATL06 quality flags
values interpretation" the correct ATL06 user_guide ranked 35th — the
top three results were ATL08 / ATL03 / ATL24 ATBDs. This is
**cross-product contamination** and the fix is surgical: pass
`source_product="ATL06"` in the request and the problem disappears.

**Second-most-fixable**: "how does X work" queries on nsidc retrieve
user_guide chunks ahead of ATBD chunks. ATBDs are where algorithms are
described; user_guides mention the algorithm names but not the method.
RRF weights cosine and lexical equally, so a user_guide chunk with many
lexical matches to "how does ATL03 geophysical corrections work" can
outrank the ATBD chapter that actually answers the question. A
**category boost** that lifts `atbd` when the query parses as a
how-does-it-work question closes this.

**docsearch version-history failures** are the same category-routing
problem in reverse: "yapc added to sliderule version" retrieved
`articles/private_clusters.html` (an article) instead of
`release_notes/release-v01-03-00.html` (the actual release entry).
Lexically "version", "release", and "yapc" appear in both, but the
user's intent is unambiguously "release_notes". A **heuristic
category auto-filter** based on the query's phrasing (look for "when
was", "added in", "version" → force `release_notes`) is the right fix.

**`docsearch/example` tutorial-vs-user-guide competition** is a
different pattern. Example queries should prefer `tutorial` chunks but
`user_guide` chunks score higher on lexical overlap for the same
subject. Same fix family (category boost on `tutorial` for queries
beginning with "example of" / "show me"), same Tier-2 lever.

**`docsearch/conceptual` earthdata miss** is unique: a legitimate
semantic-gap issue where "authentication" as a user concept pulls
security-model pages that do not describe how to authenticate earthdata
specifically. A **domain synonym map** (`earthdata ≡ .netrc ≡ CMR ≡ NASA
credentials`) is the low-cost fix. A cross-encoder reranker would also
help but is a heavier lift.

## Recommended first lever

**Cross-encoder reranker on top-20** (was previously listed as second-
choice when URL-level was the metric — now the picture has changed).
At section/page-level granularity, the dominant failure mode across
all NSIDC buckets is "right document, wrong chapter at #1." That's
precisely what cross-encoder reranking on a small candidate set
fixes: a model like `ms-marco-MiniLM-L-6-v2` (~80 MB, ~50ms latency)
re-scores top-20 with full cross-attention between query and chunk.
At our corpus size (~1,800 chunks per corpus) and top-K (20), the
reranker runs against a small candidate pool and is the single highest-
ROI change.

Expected impact: lifts NSIDC `algorithm` hit@1 from 0.00 to ~0.40-0.55,
and pulls overall hit@1 back across the 0.50 bar.

**Category auto-filter** (previously the first-choice lever based on
URL-level data) is still valuable for the docsearch side — it would
fix `docsearch/version_history` (currently 0.17 → expected 0.75+) and
`docsearch/example` failures.

Expected impact: lifts `docsearch/version_history` hit@1 from 0.17 →
0.75+ (the release_notes page becomes reachable once articles are
excluded), and `docsearch/example` from 0.80 → 0.90+.

**Note that category auto-filter does NOT help nsidc.** NSIDC has only
two categories (`atbd`, `user_guide`) and the heuristic-categorizer
move there is "atbd for how-does queries, user_guide for variable/flag
queries." That's a separate change — and it's what the second-most-
recommended lever (below) is built on.

Does not help: `nsidc/variable_lookup` and `nsidc/product_disambiguation`
(those are product mismatches, not category mismatches).

## Recommended second lever

**`source_product` filter on nsidc** + **category boost (atbd over
user_guide for "how does X work" queries)**, per the LongTermIdeas.md
Tier-2 entries. Each is a small change to `server/app.py:263-275` /
the ranking hook. Together they directly fix:

- Cross-product contamination (`nsidc/variable_lookup` and
  `nsidc/cross_product` failures where another product's ATBD outranks
  the queried product's user guide).
- Category misordering (`nsidc/algorithm` failures where user_guide
  chunks outrank ATBD chunks on algorithm-description queries).

These two together don't replace the cross-encoder reranker (which
operates one level up, on already-narrowed results) but they remove
the gross routing failures that the reranker would otherwise have to
clean up.

## Deferred

- **BM25 replacing IDF-overlap** — no specific failure in the baseline
  clearly points to it. Defer until category + source_product have been
  measured.
- **Embedder swap** — would likely help across the board but is the
  most expensive single change (full corpus rebuild). Defer.
